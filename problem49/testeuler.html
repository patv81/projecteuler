Douglas MacArthur

The general who commanded the Southwest Pacific in World War II

  • 1899 Follwing appointment by Milwaukee Congressman Theobald Otjen, enter West Point Military Academy.
  • 1901 Testifies before Congress after the hazing death of a fellow first-year cadet.
  • 1903 Graduates first in his class from West Point Military Academy.
  • 1905 Tours Far East with his parents.
  • 1906 Appointed aide to President Theodore Roosevelt.
  • 1907 Assigned to river and harbor duties at the Army engineering office in Milwaukee
  • 1913 Appointed to Army General Staff in Washington D.C under Chief Leonard Wood.
  • 1917 Assigned to Rainbow Division for France as chief of staff.
  • 1918 Decorated nine times fighting in France during WWI and promoted to General
  • 1919 Appointed Superintendent of West Point Military Academy.
  • 1922 Stationed in Philippines as Head of the New Military District of Manila.
  • 1930 Becomes Army Chief of Staff. Stationed in Washington, D.C.
  • 1936 Appointed Philippine Field Marshal.
  • 1941 FDR recalls MacArthur to active duty as U.S. Far East Commander. Japan invades Philippines and forces retreat to Bataan and Corregidor.
  • 1942 Macarthur family and command staff retreat to Australia. Curtin /MacArthur alliance formed. New Guiunea offensive begins.
  • 1943 MacArthur bypasses Rabaul.
  • 1944 Hollandia operation victory and FDR meeting in Honolulu. MacArthur promoted to five-star General.
  • 1945 MacArthur returns to the Philippines. Manila, Bataan and Corregidor recaptured. Presides over official Japanese surrender on the USS Missouri. Becomes Supreme Allied Commander on Japan.
  • 1947 MacArthur Constitution becomes law in Japan.
  • 1950 North Korea invades South Korea MacArthur becomes first United Nactions commander. Inchon landing results in capture of Seoul.
  • 1951 Truman removes MacArthur from command. Nationwide acclaim for the General. Delivers "Old Soldier" speech to Congress.
  • 1962 Farawell address to Corps of Cadets at West Point.
  • 1964 Advises President Johnson to stay out of Vietnam. Dies at Walter Reed Hospital, interred in Norfold, Virginia.
